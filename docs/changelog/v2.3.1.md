## Summary

`parseHeaders()` previously used a hand-rolled loop that tolerated at most 4 leading spaces
after a header colon (bug [M3]) and silently ignored trailing whitespace on the
`X-ARES-Token` value (bug [M14]).  RFC 7230 ┬¦3.2.3 defines OWS as zero or more SP/HTAB
characters with no limit on count.

The fix extracts two pure helpers into `src/api/http_parse_pure.h`:

- **`owsSkipLeading(p, max)`** ŌĆö advances a pointer past any leading SP/HTAB up to `max`
  characters (bounded by `HTTP_HEADER_MAX` at the call site).
- **`owsTrimTrailing(buf, len)`** ŌĆö strips trailing SP/HTAB in-place from a mutable buffer,
  returns the new length.

Both helpers are header-only (no Arduino / WiFi dependencies) and are covered by 13 new
native unit tests added to `test_api_handlers`.
